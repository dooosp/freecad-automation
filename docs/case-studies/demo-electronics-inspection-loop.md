# Demo Case Study: Electronics Inspection Loop

## Industry

Electronics assembly and end-of-line quality review for controller housings and mount brackets.

## Business Problem

The organization has recurring quality escapes between manual assembly, inspection, and launch reporting. Teams want a clearer bridge from geometry and process intent to inspection priorities.

## Data Sources

- Controller-housing and bracket examples
- Standard-doc examples for work instruction and checksheet patterns
- Inspection and quality JSON records
- Line and routing assumptions

## Virtual Twin / Simulation Angle

A DELMIA-adjacent review story can show how process steps, inspection gates, and quality evidence relate before the plant changes inspection plans or staffing. The value is in visibility and discussion discipline rather than in claiming a closed-loop execution platform.

## AI Opportunity

- Faster review-pack generation for quality meetings
- Evidence-backed triage of likely process-step causes
- Better training material for operators, quality engineers, and partner teams

## Expected KPI Impact

- Better first-pass yield conversations
- Lower repeated nonconformance loops
- Faster disposition meetings
- Stronger traceability storytelling during audits or launches

## How This Repo Can Demonstrate A Simplified Version

- Existing standard-doc examples under `docs/examples/controller-housing-eol/`
- New DELMIA-adjacent context and linkage demos
- Existing readiness and quality-linkage artifact patterns already present in `schemas/`

## Safe Positioning

This is a demo case study. It illustrates how the repo can support an inspection-loop conversation, not how a live electronics MES or DELMIA environment currently operates.
