# AI And Autonomous Manufacturing Trends

This note is written for portfolio and enablement use. It uses external industry references for context, but this repository remains a DELMIA-adjacent learning prototype rather than an autonomous manufacturing platform.

## Trend Summary

- Industrial AI is moving from dashboarding toward physical-operations assistance, including decision support, scenario review, and workforce enablement.
- Virtual-twin and digital-twin programs are increasingly tied to operational decisions, not only design visualization.
- Manufacturers are still balancing opportunity with governance, workforce readiness, and data quality constraints.

## Why It Matters For This Repo

- The repo already has a strong foundation for AI-assisted review guidance, especially around quality linkage, process planning, and readiness storytelling.
- Deterministic heuristics keep the portfolio demo auditable while still showing how AI-style assistance can accelerate review work.
- Training content matters as much as code because many manufacturing teams need a safe on-ramp before they trust higher-autonomy workflows.

## Suggested Portfolio Message

- Show how structured context, explicit routing, and traceable inspection evidence can support AI-assisted review.
- Emphasize that human manufacturing and quality owners remain accountable.
- Position future autonomy as a staged maturity path: context capture, review assistance, scenario testing, then governed automation.
